🌍 What is STEM?

STEM stands for Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics. It is an interdisciplinary approach to learning that helps students develop problem-solving, critical thinking, creativity, and innovation skills.

STEM education prepares students for future careers in technology, research, engineering, medicine, artificial intelligence, data science, and many other rapidly growing fields.

At STEM For All, our goal is to make quality STEM education accessible to every student regardless of their background.

⚡ Physics Formulas

Speed = Distance / Time

💡 Speed tells how fast an object moves.

Velocity = Displacement / Time

💡 Velocity includes both speed and direction.

Acceleration = Change in Velocity / Time

💡 Acceleration measures how quickly velocity changes.

Force = Mass × Acceleration (F = ma)

💡 Newton's Second Law explains how force affects motion.

Work = Force × Distance

💡 Work is done when a force moves an object.

Power = Work / Time

💡 Power measures how quickly work is done.

Kinetic Energy = ½mv²

💡 Energy possessed by a moving object.

Potential Energy = mgh

💡 Stored energy due to an object's position.

Momentum = Mass × Velocity

💡 A measure of how difficult it is to stop a moving object.

Density = Mass / Volume

💡 Density tells how much mass is packed into a given volume.

Pressure = Force / Area

💡 Pressure increases when the same force acts on a smaller area.

Current = Charge / Time

💡 Electric current is the flow of electric charge.

Ohm's Law: V = IR

💡 Voltage equals current multiplied by resistance.

Electric Power = VI

💡 Electrical power is the rate of energy transfer.

Frequency = 1 / Time Period

💡 Frequency tells how many cycles occur each second.

Wave Speed = Frequency × Wavelength

💡 The speed of a wave depends on its frequency and wavelength.

Weight = mg

💡 Weight is the gravitational force acting on an object.

Coulomb's Law

💡 Describes the electric force between two charged particles.

Hooke's Law

💡 The extension of a spring is proportional to the applied force.

Mirror Formula

💡 Relates object distance, image distance, and focal length.

Lens Formula

💡 Used to calculate image formation by lenses.

E = mc²

💡 Einstein's famous equation relating mass and energy.

F = ma

V = IR

PE = mgh

Momentum = mv

📖 Basic Chemistry Definitions

Atom: The smallest unit of an element that retains its chemical properties.

Molecule: A group of two or more atoms chemically bonded together.

Element: A pure substance made of only one type of atom.

Compound: A substance formed when two or more elements chemically combine.

Mixture: A combination of substances that are not chemically bonded.

Physical Change: A change that affects appearance but not chemical composition.

Chemical Change: A change that forms one or more new substances.

Reactant: A substance that takes part in a chemical reaction.

Product: A substance formed during a chemical reaction.

Acid: A substance that releases hydrogen ions in solution.

Base: A substance that can neutralize an acid.

Salt: A compound formed when an acid reacts with a base.

Solution: A homogeneous mixture of two or more substances.

Solute: The substance that gets dissolved in a solution.

Solvent: The substance that dissolves the solute.

Evaporation: The process by which a liquid changes into a gas.

Condensation: The process by which a gas changes into a liquid.

Oxidation: A process involving loss of electrons or gain of oxygen.

Reduction: A process involving gain of electrons or loss of oxygen.

Catalyst: A substance that speeds up a chemical reaction without being consumed.

Ideal Gas Law: PV = nRT

💡 Relates pressure, volume, temperature, and number of moles of a gas.

💡 Measures how acidic or basic a solution is.

💡 Concentration of a solution.

Density: Density = Mass / Volume

💡 Amount of mass packed into a given volume.

Mole Formula: Mole = Mass / Molar Mass

💡 Converts mass into number of moles.

Avogadro Constant: 6.022 × 1023

💡 Number of particles in one mole.

Atomic Number: Number of Protons

💡 Identifies an element.

Mass Number: Protons + Neutrons

💡 Total particles in the nucleus.

Boyle's Law: P₁V₁ = P₂V₂

💡 At constant temperature, pressure and volume are inversely related.

Charles's Law: V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂

💡 At constant pressure, volume is directly proportional to temperature.

Percentage Yield: (Actual Yield / Theoretical Yield) × 100

💡 Measures reaction efficiency.

Normality: Equivalents / Volume

💡 Concentration based on reactive capacity.

Mole Fraction: Moles of Component / Total Moles

💡 Used in solution chemistry.

Empirical Formula: Simplest whole-number ratio of atoms.

Molecular Formula: Actual number of atoms in a molecule.

Ionic Bond: Formed by transfer of electrons.

Covalent Bond: Formed by sharing of electrons.

Oxidation: Loss of electrons.

Reduction: Gain of electrons.
